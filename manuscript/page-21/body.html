Curious persons, who have leisure to employ in such researches, may possibly discover in the Italian writers the foundation on which our author has built. If a catastrophe, at all resembling that which he describes, is believed to have given rise to this work, it will contribute to interest the reader, and will make the “Castle of Otranto” a still more moving story.

Sonnet To The Right Honourable Lady Mary Coke.
The gentle maid, whose hapless tale  
   These melancholy pages speak;  
Say, gracious lady, shall she fail  
   To draw the tear adown thy cheek?

No; never was thy pitying breast  
   Insensible to human woes;
